ShadowPlay2.5D: A Virtual Reality Illustration Authoring Tool for Immersive Appreciation of Classical Chinese Poetry
(A) An example of Chinese shadow play: the performer animates the arm piece by fixing one control rod, and manipulating another control rod. (B) Similar to Chinese shadow play, we design ShadowPlay2.5D: users animate the image element by indicating the rigid parts, fixed points, free points first, and then manipulating one rigid part by rotating the free point with a stylus.
Abstract
Immersive experience brought by virtual reality (VR) can potentially enhance the appreciation of classical Chinese poetry, which is difficult to be described clearly by language or ordinary media. But making 3D illustrations is usually a labor-intensive and time-consuming procedure, and is hard to master for non-professionals like teachers. Motivated by the homology theory of classical Chinese poetry and painting, we propose an image-based approach of building 2.5 dimension immersive stories to visualize classical Chinese poetry. In particular, using Chinese shadow play as a metaphor, we design and implement ShadowPlay2.5D, a sketch-based authoring tool to help novices create VR illustrations of classical Chinese poetry easily. To ensure coverage of the diverse themes in Chinese poetry and preserve the sense of culture, we build a Chinese ink-painting style image repository of essential poetic elements via crowd-sourcing. To facilitate construction of 2.5D scenes, we design features that support puppet-like animation, instancing, and camera organization in a 3 dimension environment. Through two preliminary user studies, we show that ShadowPlay2.5D is easy to use by novices, and the created 2.5D stylized illustrations can bring better immersive experience for poetry appreciation. In addition, we also explore the potential usage of ShadowPlay2.5D in educational settings by interviewing two Chinese teachers, and provide insights on the bridging between VR technology and Chinese literature education.
